Chapter One: Introduction
1.1 Background
Forex (foreign exchange) is the most liquid financial market in the world and it is accessible to
anyone globally with a daily transaction volume that exceeds $7 trillion [1]. A Forex trader is a
person who operates in this market in a single day across multiple time zones. The Forex market
is open 24 hours a day, hence Forex trading is highly liquid and attracts millions of individual
traders who are looking to make money from currency fluctuations. The volatile and complex
nature of this market, however, makes it difficult to succeed and it requires more than just market
knowledge. In addition, success in Forex trading is also a matter of self-assessment, strategic
decision-making and emotional control [2].

Among the various resources that a trader might use to facilitate his performance gain, the most
frequently suggested one is a trading journal. The decision of the trader to keep a journal
documenting all his trades will grant him access to insights into the past of his trading activities
and, in turn, help him to identify new decisions that could be better than the old ones given the
situations faced. Experts such as Mark Douglas [2] and Van K. Tharp [3] maintain that, for a
trader to be self-disciplined and have a clear vision of making decisions, a trading journal is the
key thus enabling them to keep the discipline and decision-making clarity over a long period of
time. However, the challenges facing retail Forex traders extend beyond market volatility to
encompass a significant gap in access to professional coaching and performance analytics. While
institutional traders benefit from proprietary analytics platforms, dedicated performance coaches,
and structured feedback mechanisms, retail traders often rely on fragmented tools, expensive
one-on-one coaching services that can range from $200 to $500 per hour, or generic educational
content that fails to address their specific trading patterns and psychological challenges [4]. The
cost of professional trading psychology coaching places it beyond the reach of most retail
traders, particularly those in developing markets. Furthermore, existing trading journal
applications typically offer only basic logging functionality without integrated performance
analytics or access to professional coaching services. This creates a critical barrier to trader
development: individuals can record their trades but lack the expertise to interpret patterns,
identify psychological biases, or receive personalized guidance on improving their decision-
making processes. In response to these identified challenges, this project developed a
comprehensive Forex Trading Journal and Analyst Marketplace Web Application that serves as
an integrated solution bridging the gap between personal performance tracking and professional
coaching accessibility. The implemented system combines a sophisticated trade journaling
platform with advanced analytics capabilities, enabling traders to systematically record their
trades, visualize performance metrics, and identify patterns across multiple dimensions including
currency pairs, market sessions, strategies, and emotional states. Beyond self



                                                 1
-directed analysis, the platform introduces a verified analyst marketplace where retail traders can
connect with certified performance analysts and trading psychologists who offer tiered
subscription-based coaching services at accessible price points. The system implements a
rigorous analyst application and verification process administered by system administrators,
ensuring quality control in the coaching marketplace. Traders can subscribe to verified analysts
through three subscription tiers—Basic, Premium, and Elite—each offering progressive levels of
feedback frequency, personalized coaching, and advanced features such as guided journaling and
weekly performance reports. Payment processing is facilitated through integration with the
Chapa payment gateway, supporting local payment methods prevalent in East African markets.
The platform operates on a revenue-sharing model where analysts receive 70% of subscription
fees, incentivizing high-quality coaching while sustaining platform operations. This dual
approach addresses both the technical need for robust analytical tools and the human need for
expert guidance and accountability. By democratizing access to professional trading coaching
while providing enterprise-grade analytics functionality, the platform empowers retail traders to
develop the disciplined practices and receive the personalized feedback necessary for long-term
trading success. The system further incorporates gamification elements through achievement
tracking and experience points, direct messaging capabilities between traders and their
subscribed analysts, and comprehensive administrative tools for dispute resolution and system
monitoring to sustain user engagement and foster continuous improvement in trading
performance.

1.2 Statement of the Problem
In the competitive world of Forex trading, traders must continuously evaluate and improve their
performance to remain profitable. However, a significant number of traders face substantial
difficulties in tracking their trades, analyzing their strategies, and reflecting on their decision-
making processes. While trade execution is a vital skill, sustained success depends on a trader's
capacity to learn from past experiences, control emotional reactions, and adapt based on
performance trends. Unfortunately, most individual traders lack access to the necessary tools that
enable structured self-assessment and systematic performance improvement.

At present, many traders rely on manual journaling methods such as notebooks or spreadsheets
to record their trades. These approaches are often disorganized, prone to errors, and lack
essential features such as filtering, summarizing, or analyzing trade data effectively. The
psychological dimensions of trading—including emotional state, decision-making mindset, and
behavioral patterns—play a critical role in performance outcomes, yet these factors are
frequently overlooked or not tracked at all in traditional journaling methods. Traders using
unstructured logs struggle to identify their behavioral patterns, evaluate which strategies prove
most effective, or understand how their emotions influence trading results over time.

Furthermore, most digital tools that offer trade journaling and analytics—such as Edgewonk,
Myfxbook, or Tradervue—present their own limitations. These platforms are often costly, with

                                                 2
subscription fees that can exceed $50 per month, placing them beyond the financial reach of
beginner or independent traders. Additionally, many of these tools lack integration with regional
payment systems, making them inaccessible to traders in developing markets. The geographic
and technical limitations of existing solutions create barriers that prevent traders from accessing
the performance tracking tools they need to develop their skills systematically [5].

Beyond the limitations of tracking tools, retail traders face an even more significant challenge:
limited access to professional coaching and personalized feedback on their trading performance.
While institutional traders benefit from dedicated performance coaches and structured
mentorship programs, retail traders must either navigate the market independently or pay
prohibitively expensive fees for one-on-one coaching sessions. The absence of affordable,
structured coaching platforms creates a critical gap in trader development. Traders may
successfully record their performance data but lack the expertise to interpret complex patterns,
identify psychological biases, or receive actionable guidance on improving their decision-making
processes. This coaching accessibility gap perpetuates the cycle of trader failure, as individuals
cannot access the professional insights necessary to translate raw performance data into
meaningful behavioral change.

This project addressed these multifaceted challenges through the development and
implementation of a comprehensive Forex Trading Journal and Analyst Marketplace Web
Application. The system tackled the trade tracking problem by providing robust data entry
mechanisms supporting both manual input and CSV import, comprehensive filtering and
analytics capabilities, and psychological tracking features that capture emotional states alongside
technical trade data. The platform addressed the coaching accessibility gap by establishing a
verified analyst marketplace connecting traders with certified performance analysts through
tiered subscription plans, integrated payment processing via the Chapa gateway supporting East
African payment methods, and a revenue-sharing economic model that makes professional
coaching financially accessible to retail traders while creating viable income opportunities for
qualified analysts. By integrating structured trade logging, advanced performance analytics, and
accessible professional coaching within a single platform, the implemented system provides a
holistic solution enabling traders to bridge the gap between trade execution and long-term
performance improvement.

1.3 Objectives
1.3.1 General Objective
The primary objective of this project is to develop a web-based Forex Trading Journal and
Analyst Marketplace that bridges the gap between individual performance tracking and
professional coaching. The system aims to facilitate data-driven decision-making by enabling
traders to record, organize, and analyze their trading activities through a structured journaling
interface. Furthermore, the project seeks to democratize access to professional mentorship by

                                                3
connecting retail traders with verified performance analysts through a secure, subscription-based
marketplace. Ultimately, the system provides a holistic platform for continuous trader
development, combining self-assessment analytics with expert feedback to foster disciplined
trading habits.

1.3.2 Specific Objectives
Trade Management and Journaling

      To design a comprehensive trade logging system that supports both manual data entry
       and bulk CSV import mechanisms for efficient record-keeping.

      To implement detailed trade attributes including technical parameters, strategy tagging,
       and psychological state tracking to capture the full context of each trade.

      To enable the attachment of visual evidence such as trade screenshots and chart links,
       facilitating thorough post-trade review and analysis.

      To develop an interactive analytics dashboard utilizing Chart.js to visualize key

      To implement automated reporting features that generate and distribute weekly

1.4 Methodology
   • The development of the Forex Trading Journal and Analyst Marketplace adhered to a
   structured research and development methodology suitable for web-based system creation.
   This approach ensured that the project addressed verified user needs while maintaining high
   standards of software engineering throughout the lifecycle. The methodology encompasses
   distinct phases of data collection, system development modeling, and architectural design
   patterning.

1.4.1 Data Collection
   • To determine the functional requirements and identify current market gaps, the project
   employed a multi-faceted data collection strategy.

   • Document Analysis A systematic review of existing trading journal applications and
   financial software was conducted to evaluate current industry standards. This analysis
   highlighted common limitations in existing tools, particularly the lack of integrated
   performance feedback and psychological tracking features. These findings served as the
   baseline for defining the system's unique value proposition.

   • Interviews Structured interviews were conducted with active retail forex traders to gather
   primary data regarding their daily workflows and pain points. Integrating qualitative
   feedback from potential users exposed specific challenges related to accountability and the
   high cost of professional coaching. This direct engagement ensured that the system's features
   were aligned with actual user needs rather than theoretical assumptions.

   • Online Research Extensive research into trading forums and psychological literature
   provided context on the behavioral aspects of trading failure. Understanding the
   psychological barriers to consistent profitability informed the design of the emotional
   tracking and analyst feedback modules. This broader market analysis validated the demand
   for an affordable, community-driven coaching solution.

1.4.2 System Development Model: Agile
   • The project utilized the Agile software development methodology, prioritizing iterative
   progress and flexibility over rigid long-term planning. The development lifecycle was
   divided into two-week sprints, each focusing on delivering specific, testable components of
   the system. This approach allowed for continuous refinement of features based on immediate
   testing feedback and evolving requirements. The iterative nature of Agile ensured that critical

                                                5
    functions, such as the trade logging engine and analyst marketplace, were robustly tested
    before integration.

1.4.3 Design Pattern: MVC
    • The system architecture follows the Model-View-Controller (MVC) design pattern, a
    widely accepted standard for modern web applications. This differentiation separates the
    application's internal data handling (Model) from the user interface (View) and the logic that
    connects them (Controller). By decoupling these components, the architecture supports
    modular development, making the codebase easier to maintain and scale. This structure also
    facilitated parallel development, allowing the backend logic and frontend interface to
    progress simultaneously without conflict.

1.4.4 Programming Languages and Tools
    • The technology stack was selected to ensure system scalability, security, and responsive
    performance across devices.

    • Backend Development The server-side logic is powered by the Laravel PHP framework,
    chosen for its robust security features and elegant syntax. Laravel handles core functions
    such as user authentication, database management, and API routing. Its ecosystem enabled
    the seamless integration of complex features like PDF report generation and automated email
    scheduling.

    • Frontend Development The user interface was constructed using HTML, CSS, and the
    TailwindCSS framework to ensure a modern, responsive design. JavaScript facilitates

    dynamic interactions, while Chart.js powers the interactive analytics dashboard. This
    combination ensures that the application remains fast and accessible on both desktop and
    mobile devices.

    complex relationships between users, trades, and subscription records. The system integrates
    Chapa for secure payment processing, enabling local currency transactions for the analyst

    charting tools within the platform environment.

1.6.2 Limitations of the Project
While the implemented system addresses critical gaps in retail trader development, it operates
within specific technical and functional boundaries that define its intended use case.

Functional Boundaries The platform serves as a post-trade analysis and coaching tool rather
than an active trading terminal. It does not connect to brokerage APIs for live trade execution,
nor does it provide real-time market data feeds or economic calendars. Users must continue
using their existing trading platforms for market analysis and trade placement, treating this
system as a complementary performance review tool. This deliberate separation ensures the
project remains focused on its core value proposition without the regulatory complexities of
handling live trading operations.

Technical Constraints As a web-based application, the system requires active internet
connectivity and does not support offline functionality. The current version calculates
performance metrics primarily in USD and pip values, without multi-currency portfolio support
for trading accounts. While the Chapa payment integration supports local currencies, the
journaling component itself maintains a single-currency accounting structure. The user interface
is currently available exclusively in English, limiting accessibility for non-English speaking
traders.

Institutional Limitations The system is designed for individual retail traders rather than
institutional trading firms or multi-user trading teams. It lacks features such as multi-trader
portfolio aggregation, institutional compliance reporting, or team collaboration tools. These
deliberate scope limitations keep the platform focused on its primary audience while maintaining
manageable development complexity.

2.1 Introduction
This chapter examines the business domain and requirements for the Forex Trading Journal and
Analyst Marketplace. Retail traders face significant challenges due to inconsistent journaling,
limited coaching access, and a lack of integrated analytics. Most rely on fragmented solutions
like spreadsheets or generic note-taking apps, which lack essential features such as automated
data visualization and psychological analysis—critical factors for profitability [2], [3].

The implemented solution addresses these gaps with a web-based platform integrating
journaling, analytics, and professional coaching. The system supports three user roles: Traders

architecture creates a collaborative environment where data-driven self-assessment is enhanced

processes. It details current trading limitations, functional and non-functional requirements, and

2.2 Business Area Analysis
2.2.1 Detailed Analysis
The business domain of retail Forex trading encompasses a diverse ecosystem of individual
traders, educational content providers, commercial journaling platforms, and informal
mentorship networks. Retail traders operate independently without the institutional support
systems available to professional trading firms, creating a significant gap in access to
performance tracking tools and qualified coaching services. The global Forex market's 24-hour
trading cycle and high leverage accessibility have democratized market participation, yet the
majority of retail traders fail to achieve sustained profitability due to psychological factors,
inadequate risk management, and the absence of structured performance review processes [1],
[2].

Current market research indicates that approximately 70-80% of retail Forex traders lose money
over extended periods, a failure rate attributable not primarily to market unpredictability but to
behavioral and disciplinary deficiencies. Successful institutional traders benefit from mandatory
trade logging, regular performance reviews with supervisors, and access to trading
psychologists—resources entirely absent from the retail trading environment. The few
commercial platforms that attempt to bridge this gap either charge prohibitive subscription fees,
lack psychological tracking features, or fail to integrate professional coaching within their

                                               16
ecosystems. This market failure creates a clear opportunity for an integrated solution that
combines affordable journaling tools with accessible professional mentorship.

The business case for the Forex Trading Journal and Analyst Marketplace rests on three
fundamental value propositions. First, the platform reduces friction in trade logging through
automated calculations, CSV import capabilities, and intuitive interfaces that encourage
consistent usage. Second, the integrated analytics engine transforms raw trade data into
actionable insights through visualization and pattern identification, enabling traders to diagnose
performance issues independently. Third, the analyst marketplace creates a bilateral value
exchange where traders access affordable coaching while qualified analysts monetize their
expertise through scalable subscription services. This three-pronged approach addresses both the
technical limitations of existing tools and the economic barriers preventing retail traders from
accessing professional guidance.

3.1 Introduction
System design translates Chapter 2's functional requirements into a concrete architectural
blueprint for implementation. This chapter presents the structural and behavioral design
decisions for the Forex Trading Journal and Analyst Marketplace system, balancing technical
concerns (performance, scalability, security) with organizational objectives (maintainability,
extensibility, development efficiency).

The design employs a layered architecture separating presentation, application logic, and data
persistence tiers. Laravel's MVC pattern provides the structural foundation, enhanced with
Repository, Service Layer, and Adapter patterns. External integrations (Chapa payment gateway,
Chart.js) use adapter interfaces to maintain loose coupling.

Design artifacts include component diagrams, deployment diagrams, ER diagrams, and detailed
database specifications providing the technical blueprint for Chapter 4's implementation phase.

3.2 Purpose of the System
The system serves dual purposes: providing retail traders with sophisticated performance
tracking tools and creating a marketplace connecting traders with qualified coaching
professionals. It addresses gaps in existing journaling solutions that lack emotional tracking,
coaching integration, or affordable pricing for developing markets.

For analysts, the platform enables monetizing coaching expertise through scalable subscription
models rather than expensive one-on-one consulting. The platform handles payment processing,

Technically, the system demonstrates modern web technologies delivering complex, multi-tenant

role-based access control, and responsive interfaces.

Gate and Policy systems enforce role-based permissions. Middleware guards protect route

only subscribed traders, admins have elevated privileges.

Data Encryption and Privacy

TLS 1.2+ encrypts all HTTP traffic. Database field-level encryption protects sensitive data. File
uploads undergo type/size validation. CSRF tokens protect state-modifying requests.

Payment Security

Chapa's hosted checkout collects payment data directly—application receives only transaction
tokens. Webhook signature verification validates authentic callbacks. Transaction IDs enable
audit trails without exposing financial data.

Laravel's request lifecycle processes HTTP requests through middleware pipeline, routing,
controller invocation, and response generation.

Request Initiation and Routing

Web server delegates to Laravel bootstrap. Router matches URLs to controllers. Middleware
executes authentication, CSRF validation, logging.

Controller Orchestration

Controllers coordinate by delegating to service classes. They validate inputs, invoke services,
construct responses. Dependency injection auto-resolves dependencies.

5.1 Conclusion
The development of the Forex Trading Journal and Analyst Marketplace successfully addressed
the critical gap in the Ethiopian trading ecosystem by delivering a localized, technology-driven
solution. The project achieved its primary objective of transitioning traders from manual, error-
prone spreadsheet tracking to an automated, analytical platform.

By integrating a sophisticated journaling module with a secure analyst marketplace, the system
has created a symbiotic environment where novice traders can learn from verified experts while
rigorous performance tracking ensures transparency. The successful integration of the Chapa
payment gateway solved the significant challenge of local payment accessibility, enabling
seamless monetization for analysts and secure transactions for subscribers.

The technical implementation, utilizing Laravel 12.0 and modern web technologies, resulted in a
robust, responsive, and secure application. Testing confirmed that the system meets all functional
requirements for trade management, analytics visualization, and subscription handling. In
conclusion, the project provides a scalable, sustainable platform that professionalizes forex
trading in Ethiopia and empowers the local trading community with data-driven decision-making
tools.

5.2 Recommendation
Based on the project findings and future potential, the following recommendations are aimed at
future developers, stakeholders, and the trading community:

   1. Mobile Application Development: It is recommended to develop a native mobile
      application (iOS/Android) in the next phase. While the web platform is responsive, a
      native app would enable push notifications for real-time trade alerts and offline
      journaling capabilities, significantly enhancing user engagement.

   2. Automated Broker Synchronization: Future iterations should prioritize integrating
      APIs from major trading platforms (MetaTrader 4/5, cTrader). Automating data import
      would eliminate manual entry errors entirely and provide real-time account monitoring,
      further reducing the friction for user adoption.

   3. Advanced Social Trading Features: Expanding the marketplace into a full "Copy
      Trading" ecosystem is recommended. Allowing subscribers to automatically replicate
      analyst trades would significantly increase platform value. This requires navigating
      complex regulatory frameworks and advanced low-latency infrastructure.
